Creating Engineers to leadership through the PAF foundation

Problems look mighty small from 150 miles up

Posted by Ms. Saalai Manika on May 21, 2022

Ten (10) students were selected through the office of H.E. Vice President for fullyfunded scholarship facilitated by Periyar African Foundation (PAF) for engineering degrees in India through “Founders scholarship at SRM University A.P” which covered tuition, hostel (Boarding & Lodging) and healthcare. The Ghana Education Trust Fund (GETFund) offered periodic stipends for their upkeep. These 10 students pursued general science at the Senior High School level and demonstrated excellence in their academics.

An orientation program was organized on the 17th and 18th of September 2017, by Mr. KC Ezhilarasan – Founder/Chairman of Periyar African Foundation (PAF) in unison with Mr. Issahaku Dahamani and Dr. Abudulai Sulemana – representatives of the office of H.E Vice President and Mr. Owusu - alumni of SRM Chennai informed the students on the living conditions, opportunities, and how to excel in the new environment. The students after preparation reported to SRM University A.P on 24th of September 2017. They were received by Mr. Ish Barga - then Head of International Students Relation.

A WhatsApp platform was formed to communicate with the students frequently, with Mr. Issahaku Dahamani, Dr. Abudulai Sulemana, Mr. KC Ezhilarasan and Ms. Saalai Manikam on the platform to ensure that day-to-day wellbeing and matters relating to academics were effectively attended to on time. Mr. Issahaku Dahamani and Dr. Abubulai Sulemana effectively communicated with GETFund to ensure timely payments of stipends.

Visits to meet the students by Periyar African Foundation represented by Ms. Saalai Manikam
First Visit
2nd December 2017, a meeting was convened on the following day, with the university authority including Dr. D Gunasekaran - then Registrar, Mr. Ish Bagga, and Mr. Rana Vikram Singh -then Head of Students Affairs where students gave feedback and suggestions on their living conditions with the authority accepting to implement those feedbacks. Ms. Saalai Manikam, Mr. Vikram, and the students toured notable sites within the state beginning with the local farmland of Jute, Cotton, and Sugarcane where she emphasized the importance of Agriculture, exports, and entrepreneurship. They moved on to the Buddha Temple and then to the Undavalli Caves. All was purposed at giving the students a broadening experience.
Second visit
13th April 2018, where she spent time with the student and witnessed the 2018 University Cultural festival which took place in the university. Her visit was purposed on inspecting implemented suggestions on living conditions of the students.
Third visit
8th to 10th March 2019, She celebrated Women’s Day with 5 of the students at Chennai. She toured with them, the city's notable places including Mahabalipuram, Chennai port, and a few notable recreational centers. She engaged the students in the evenings on their future prospective career paths with counselling on how they can achieve those goals. She organized a meeting on 9th March 2019, with Dr. K. Veeramani, Mentor to Periyar African Foundation, who interacted with the students about their experience of the Indian social systems and education.
